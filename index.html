Historical Places In India !...

.......India Gate........

  1. The India Gate is formerly known as the All India War Memorial.

  2. Designed By: Edwin Lutyens.

  3. Inaugurated By: Lord Irwin

  4. Illuminated Time: 19:00 to 21:30 (Every Evening).

  5. In 2017, the India Gate was twinned with the Arch of Remembrance in Leicester, England.

  6. Height:42 Meter

  7. Announcement: On 21 January 2022, Prime Minister Narendra Modi announced that a statue of Subhas Chandra Bose would be installed in the canopy at India Gate.

.......Amrit Sar.......

  1. Historical Name:Rāmdāspur and colloquially as Ambarsar.

  2. Located At:Punjab.

  3. Amritsar is economic capital of Punjab.

  4. According to the historical Sikh records, the site was chosen by Guru Amar Das and called Guru Da Chakk.

  5. According to 2011 Census of India, Sikhism and Hinduism were the main religions of the Amritsar city at 48% and 49.36% of the population.

  6. Amritsar is the second-largest city and district of Punjab. It is also one of the fastest-growing cities of Punjab.

.........Taj Mahal.........

  1. Developed By: It was commissioned in 1631 by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan.

  2. Location :Agra,Utterpradesh.

  3. Arra :17 Hecters.

  4. The Taj Mahal incorporates and expands on design traditions of Indo-Islamic and earlier Mughal architecture.

  5. The imperial court documenting Shah Jahan's grief after the death of Mumtaz Mahal illustrates the love story held as the inspiration for the Taj Mahal.

  6. The garden uses raised pathways that divide each of the four-quarters of the garden.

.......Qutub Minar.......

  1. Developed By: Qutub Minar was begun after the Quwwat-ul-Islam Mosque, which was started around 1192 by Qutb-ud-din Aibak, first ruler of the Delhi Sultanate.

  2. Height: 72.5m.

  3. The Qutb Minar consists of five stories of red and grey sandstone.

  4. The construction of the Qutb Minar was planned and financed by the Ghurids, who emigrated to India and brought Islam with them.

  5. he Qutb Minar, also spelled Qutub Minar and Qutab Minar, is a minaret and "victory tower" that forms part of the Qutb complex.

  3. On 15 August 1947, the first Prime Minister of India, Jawaharlal Nehru, raised the Indian flag above the Lahori Gate.
